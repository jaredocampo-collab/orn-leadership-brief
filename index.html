Three things leadership needs to know this month

Ordinances performed are outpacing new ORNs

The current supply stands at 72.19M, down from 72.64M on January 1. Our automation effort and 13 User Activity initiatives are the primary vehicles for closing this gap in the second half of 2026.

User Activity Goal Insights

User Activity ORNs never peaked above 1M in any month in 2025 — expecting a 2.5X increase without a clear path was not realistic. The goal was based on numbers that included system processes, policy changes, and RLL that were not User Activity. Our current trajectory is 11.6M. The overall 32M goal is not changing — we need to shift more of it to the automated pipeline.

ORN Pipeline Progress

A new AI-based full-text transcription system went live June 1. Automated ATB processing has loaded all historical records and Family Tree data into the Reference Tree for evaluation. End-to-end testing is targeted for October.

Executive Summary · May 2026 · Execution Grade: B

For May, the ORN SOC execution grade is B. The committee is focused and executing well — the pipeline is advancing and key milestones have been delivered. Results against annual goals are behind pace, but two structural factors explain the gap: a 23M User Activity goal set on numbers we now understand were overstated, and an automated pipeline that is on track but not yet producing.

At the end of July, a new reporting data lake will be ready to consume data — allowing the organization to see for the first time ORN attribution by source, removal of duplicate data, and many other insights that will give leadership accurate data to act on. In August, the automated pipeline completes its full data reload and external source testing begins. The second half of 2026 is where the pipeline delivers. It's a great day to be at FamilySearch!

4.7M YTD — 20% of the 23M annual goal, 49% of the 9.6M expected by May 31. Two structural factors explain the gap, not a demand problem. Leadership recalibration on organic growth expectations is underway.

User Activity is at 4.7M YTD — 20% of the 23M annual goal, and 49% of the 9.6M expected by May 31. We now believe two factors explain this gap.

Record visibility constraints: Visible records have remained flat at 4.1 billion for 10+ months, largely due to the Themis rights changeover restricting more records than anticipated. Hints generate automatically from newly published records — the real forward lever is Content Access publication, not a separate hint release process.

Prior-year distortion: Non-replicable measurement spikes in 2025 — including the 110-year expiry cohort, bulk standardization runs, and qualification caching — inflated year-over-year comparisons and the 2026 goal, creating an artificial performance gap. User Activity ORNs never peaked above 1M in any single month of 2025.

A question was raised about whether Forest Builders may be choosing to upload names to User Trees rather than the FamilySearch Tree. The data does not support this — Forest Builders are adding 19.5M names to the FamilySearch Tree YTD, while also uploading 30.2M names to User Trees. These are not competing activities; Forest Builders are the most active segment in both. We currently have no way to attribute ORNs from User Trees — the data platform and Reference Tree consumption work must be complete before we can provide that reporting. Initiatives 9 and 10 in the active project list below are directly targeted at closing this conversion gap.

What we learned this month

1
The 23M User Activity goal was set in error — this was a major topic at the June ORN SOC Leadership Meeting
Brad Morgan, BI Manager — June 11, 2026
Correction
What was believed

The 23M goal was set as an achievable stretch for 2026 User Activity based on 2025 production data.

What the data shows

ORNs from User Activity never peaked above 1M in any month in 2025 — expecting a 2.5X increase to 1.92M/month without a clear path was not realistic. The 23M was based on numbers that included significant contributions from system processes (110-year expiry, qualification caching), policy changes, and RLL — none of which are User Activity and none of which are repeatable. Current trajectory without interventions: 11.6M.

Action being taken

The ORN SOC discussed shifting goal allocations to make User Activity more attainable and rely more on the automated processes now being built. No plans to change the overall 32M goal. We still need to hit that. This requires a leadership decision — see the Questions tab.

2
New learnings for User Activity
ORN SOC Business Review · June 2026 data analysis
New insight
What this corrects

A question was raised about whether Forest Builders may be shifting activity to User Trees instead of the FamilySearch Tree, thereby reducing ORN production. The data does not support this. User Trees show 30.2M names added YTD by Forest Builders — however, we currently have no way to attribute ORNs from User Trees. The data platform and Reference Tree consumption work must be complete before that reporting is possible. User activity adding qualified names has actually been growing since September 2025, at approximately 60% faster than new ORNs produced.

Why this matters and where to find details

Hints are automatic — the lever is record publishing rate, not a scheduled hint release
Brad Morgan, BI Manager — corrects framing raised by Devin Ashby and Trish Melander in the June 3 Business Review
Correction
What was said

"We haven't had a batch hint release since August 2025" — framing this as a discrete event that could be scheduled and delivered to restore Forest Builder productivity.

What is actually true

Hints are generated automatically whenever new records are published to the platform. There is no separate "release" to trigger. The rate of hint supply for Forest Builders is directly tied to the rate at which Content Access publishes records. Visible records have been flat at 4.1 billion for 10+ months due to the Themis rights changeover. This is the real constraint. A hint refresh is underway (data exports from CDS, SLS, Trees, and LLS in progress; estimated completion July–August) incorporating all record updates since 2024.

The cross-SOC ask

Record publication will resume as new redaction capabilities come online — new collection data is flowing, but only content meeting current privacy requirements is being published. What the ORN SOC needs from Content Access is collection optimization data: which collections to reprocess first, and what ORN yield we can expect from each. This will allow the ORN SOC to prioritize hint refresh work and set realistic expectations for the User Activity channel through the second half of 2026.

4
Novel name additions are flat — duplicate rates are rising as the tree fills in
Brad Morgan, BI Manager — June 2026 analysis
Emerging risk
What the data shows

While total names added continues to increase, novel names — those not already in the tree — remain flat. This suggests our public and member users are adding a higher number of duplicates by percentage over time. Only novel names can become new ORNs. As the FamilySearch Tree fills in, users increasingly add ancestors already present.

Why this matters

This is a structural trend that will worsen without active deduplication investment. A Duplication Resolution Project proposal is now in hand; cross-ET coordination is required. This is also a reason the automated pipeline — which matches and deduplicates at machine scale — is essential to long-term supply sustainability beyond 2026.

Questions for this meeting

These are not rhetorical. They are questions where the answer changes what the organization does. The ORN SOC is bringing them to leadership because the decisions involved go beyond what the SOC can make on its own — and because getting them right matters for 2026 and for the path to 200M by 2030. Question 1
Should we reallocate the 23M User Activity goal internally — adjusting it to reflect the realistic 11.6M trajectory and adding the difference to the automated pipeline goal — while keeping the total 32M unchanged?
The 23M goal was based on numbers that included system events not caused by human activity. User Activity has never exceeded 1M names in a single month. The SOC's honest trajectory is 11.6M. Keeping 23M on the books creates the appearance of a larger gap than actually exists in human-driven activity, and may draw resources toward interventions that cannot close a structurally misstated goal. The 32M total goal is not proposed for change — we still need to hit that.
Stakes: How we answer this shapes how every future leadership conversation about "organic growth" is framed — and where the organization's energy and accountability go in the second half of 2026.
Question 2
Which collections should Content Access publish first — and what is the expected ORN yield from each?
Record publication will resume as new redaction capabilities come online. The ORN SOC needs collection optimization data — a prioritized view of which collections to reprocess and republish, paired with projected ORN yield per collection. This insight is needed by September/October to allow the ORN team to align hint refresh work, set realistic second-half expectations for User Activity, and plan accordingly. Without it, we cannot prioritize effectively or give leadership an accurate forward projection for the User Activity channel.
Stakes: The optimization data directly determines how much of the User Activity gap can be closed in the second half of 2026. Without it by September/October, planning for year-end and 2027 is operating without a key input.
