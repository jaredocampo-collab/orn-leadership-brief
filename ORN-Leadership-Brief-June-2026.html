Executive Summary Statement

For May, we give ourselves a B+. We are producing ORNs and making strong pipeline progress. Updated measurements show 10.4M YTD (33% of goal) with supply now 1.5M above our starting point—a significant improvement. While measurement systems continue to improve (July internal reconciliation, August data lake), the trajectory is positive and our automation effort remains on track for H2 delivery.

Overall status remains yellow. The core challenge remains generating sufficient gross supply at a rate that outpaces ordinance performance. The ACE transcription system went live June 1 (Rock #1 complete). The next 60 days — June and July — are critical. We expect to advance the ORN reporting PoC into production, complete the runtime system, and confirm what is coming from Content Access publication that could drive organic growth.

It is a great day to be at FamilySearch.

Ownership: Content Access SOC · Director: Stephen Valentine
What we need: Collection optimization data and ORN yield projections
High
We have learned an important dependency: we need optimization data from Content Access to know which collections should be reprocessed first and what ORN yield we can expect from them. Record republication will resume as new redaction capabilities come online for collections that have been on the site for some time. New collection data is flowing, but it does not automatically trigger republication of all content — only content that meets current privacy requirements is being published. Hints generate automatically from newly published records, so the rate of record publication directly shapes Forest Builder productivity and new ORN production.
